There is a structural mismatch between the phenomenon and the method. The cases that reach a clinic involve transcripts that run to hundreds of pages over days. The studies that inform policy involve a handful of turns in a controlled setting.

## The mismatch

A single-turn vignette can establish that a model *can* produce a harmful response. It cannot establish how a vulnerable person and a model co-evolve a belief over seventy-two hours.

## Why it's hard to fix

Simulating sustained interaction is genuinely difficult, and the obvious shortcuts introduce their own artifacts. This is the methodological problem our group is most focused on right now.
